What Is The Aggregate?

The aggregate is the loose granular materials, which play the role of the skeleton or filling the role in concrete. In the conditions of not affect the performance of concrete, we can add the aggregate in the concrete as much as possible. It can reduce the cost of concrete. With the development of foundation construction, the aggregate role is bigger and bigger.

How About The Aggregate Application?

Aggregate is the main raw material of concrete, and has the skeleton and supporting role in building. In the mixing water, cement by mixing, into a thin paste, if not aggregate words, it will not be formed, will lead to use. So we can say that the aggregate is very important in building materials. Application of aggregate in the civil engineering: cement concrete, asphalt concrete, road infrastructure, railway ballast, mortar etc.. There are about 2 billion tons of dosages every year. The aggregate can provide the good stability which is better than the concrete, and the better durability which is better than the cement stone. It can reduce the contraction, inhibit the crack propagation, the force transmission, reduce the hydration heat, and wearing resistance effects.

What Is The Aggregate Classification?

From the aggregate sources, it can be divided into the natural rock aggregate, the artificial aggregate and the recycled aggregate. The former is composed of natural rock, such as sand, pebble, gravel etc. The thermal processing aggregate has expanded shale, expanded vermiculite; industrial by-product slag, slag, powder residue etc. The latter has the broken concrete and broken clay brick etc..

From the aggregate particle size, it can be divided into the coarse aggregate and fine aggregate. Coarse aggregate’s rock particle size is greater than 4.75mm, such as gravel, gravel etc. Fine aggregate’s rock particles is less than 4.75mm, such as river sand, mountain sand, sand etc..

From the density of aggregate, it can be divided into the common aggregate, lightweight aggregate and heavy aggregate. The common aggregate’s density is in2500 - 2700kg/m3. Lightweight aggregate’s density is in 0~1000 kg/m3, such as ceramic, calcined shale, expanded vermiculite, expanded perlite, foam particles. Heavy aggregate’s density is between 3500 - 4000 kg/m3, such as iron ore, barite etc..
